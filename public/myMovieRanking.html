The Best Movies According to Brook Haile

My top 3 movies of all times:

The Passion Of Christ

As a follower of the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church who is studying to become a deacon, The Passion of the Christ resonates with my spiritual journey. The film’s portrayal of suffering, sacrifice, and unwavering obedience reflects the foundation of the faith that has shaped Ethiopia for centuries. Learning about the devotion of our ancient Christian kings and queens—how they governed with righteousness, sought justice, and placed their trust in God above worldly power—has strengthened my understanding of what it means to live a life centered on Christ.

Many of these leaders, such as King Ezana, Emperor Amda Seyon, Emperor Naod, Emperor Dawit, Emperor Kaleb, Emperor Zera Yacob, and Emperor Lebna Dengel, were known not only for their leadership but also for their humility and reverence for God. Some even left their thrones behind to live in monastic communities, choosing prayer and service over earthly authority. Reading their histories has inspired me to reflect on their love and devotion to our Lord, Adonai Iyesus Christos, and has renewed my own commitment to walk in the faith of these ancient fathers.

Vigin River

Virgin River is one of my top three favorite shows because it highlights the beauty of starting over and finding healing through community and compassion. The characters face real struggles—loss, grief, uncertainty—but they continue to move forward by leaning on faith, friendship, and hope. That journey of rebuilding life after hardship is something I deeply connect with on a personal level.

I also appreciate how the show portrays humility and service in everyday life. The way Mel dedicates herself to caring for others and how the people of Virgin River support one another reflects values that matter to me—kindness, patience, and commitment to doing what is right even when it is difficult. It reminds me that strength often shows itself in quiet acts of love and sacrifice.

Most of all, Virgin River gives a sense of peace. The slower pace of life, the natural surroundings, and the sincere relationships among the characters make it a comforting and uplifting story to watch. It’s not just entertainment—it’s a reminder that healing, purpose, and belonging can be found when people choose compassion and stand by one another through every season of life.

Space 1999

Space: 1999 is one of my favorite classic science fiction series because it combines imagination with thoughtful storytelling about humanity’s place in the universe. The show presents not only futuristic technology and space exploration, but also deep questions about survival, purpose, and the unknown. Watching the Moon and its inhabitants travel through unfamiliar galaxies creates a sense of wonder that continues to inspire my curiosity about science and discovery.

I am drawn to how the characters must rely on discipline, teamwork, and moral judgment while facing unpredictable environments and challenges. Their mission is not just technical—it becomes a test of resilience, leadership, and unity in the face of isolation and uncertainty. This reminds me that progress often requires both knowledge and strong character.

The series also explores themes of responsibility and the consequences of human decisions. As they encounter new worlds and situations, the crew must constantly weigh risks and choose paths that preserve life and dignity. That balance between exploration and ethical responsibility gives the show a meaningful depth beyond its science fiction setting.

Above all, Space: 1999 captures a vision of perseverance in the midst of the unknown. Even when the future seems uncertain, the characters continue forward with courage and hope. That spirit of endurance and commitment to their shared mission makes the series timeless and one that I continue to appreciate.
