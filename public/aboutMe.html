About Me

I was born and raised in Ethiopia in a small city called Debre Zeit (Bishoftu). Growing up in a disciplined and values-driven environment, I attended Catholic school where I developed a strong foundation in integrity, responsibility, and perseverance. My passion for problem-solving and infrastructure led me to pursue engineering, and I graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering. Shortly after graduation, I began working for the national government utility company as a Distribution Construction and Operations Engineer I/II, where I gained hands-on experience in power systems, field construction, grid reliability, and utility operations over three years.

In October 2011, I moved to the United States through the Diversity Visa program, determined to expand my professional growth in advanced infrastructure systems. I briefly worked with Pepco contractors (Henkels & McCoy) before joining the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA). Over the course of 11 years, I progressed from High Voltage Technician D to C and ultimately to B Mechanic Level. During that time, I developed deep expertise in high-voltage systems, substation equipment, preventive and corrective maintenance, safety protocols, troubleshooting, and mission-critical power environments. After formally resigning to explore relocation opportunities, I remained in the DMV area and began strategically preparing for the next stage of my career.

Today, I am focused on transitioning into advanced power and critical infrastructure engineering roles within data centers, national security facilities, or transportation power systems. I am actively strengthening my technical stack in Linux, Python, AWS, networking, web development, and advanced electrical science. I have successfully passed interviews for a Data Center Facilities Technician Level III role at Google and am awaiting a final decision. I have also been selected to interview for AWS Engineering Operations Technician roles. Additionally, I passed interviews for a High Voltage Technician position with the NSA and am currently awaiting TS/SCI clearance review. My long-term goal is to serve in a full power engineering role—whether in hyperscale data centers, federal facilities, Amtrak substations, or similar mission-critical environments—and to pursue a Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering. I am driven, adaptable, technically curious, and committed to building resilient infrastructure that powers modern society.
